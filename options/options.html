Lazy Remix Filter

Filter YouTube and YouTube Music rows by title (and optionally channel text). Everything runs locally in your browser—nothing is sent to a server.

Filtering

Choose how rows that match your patterns are treated.

Can catch more remixes; may occasionally match channels you did not intend.

Counter

You can dismiss the chip with the × on the page. Turn this option off and on in settings to show it again without reloading.

Block list

One phrase per line. Matching is case-insensitive unless you use regex (see below).

Advanced: regex and patterns

Lines starting with regex: use a JavaScript regex (e.g. regex:slowed\s*\+\s*reverb). You can also use /pattern/flags.

Allow list

If any line matches the title or channel (when enabled), the row is never hidden.

Backup

Export or import your settings as JSON.

Known limitations
  • Works on youtube.com (including www, apex, and m.), and music.youtube.com. It does not run on YouTube Studio, embedded players in iframes on other sites, or other YouTube subdomains unless added to the extension.
  • Rows inside closed shadow DOM cannot be seen by the script; some UI experiments may be missed until selectors are updated.
  • Detection is keyword-based; some remix titles slip through, and rare false positives can occur.
  • YouTube may change its page structure; if filtering stops after an update, the extension may need fixes.
  • Filtering applies to visible lists and grids, not to audio already playing in the background only.
  • Built with standard Manifest V3 chrome.* APIs for compatibility with Chrome, Edge, Brave, Opera, Vivaldi, Arc, and other Chromium-based browsers.
